Website Critiques

Questions:

  1. What was your first impression of this site?
  2. How does this site organize the flow of information? (Do they have links on the left and text on the right? Some other scheme? No scheme?)
  3. Does the site structure make sense or does it confuse you? Why?
  4. Does the site graphics/layout/multimedia enhance the site or detract from it? Why?
  5. How long does it take for the home page to load? Was the load time reasonable?
  6. Was the text color and size readable? If a background color or image was used, did it enhance or detract from the site?
  7. Did the site have a naviagtion bar or other navigation tool? Was the navigation well done
  8. Did the site have a color scheme or look and feel that was repeated on all the pages?
  9. How does the site respond to smaller window sizes (responsive design)?
  10. What does this site offer that was unusual or very well done?

Website 1 Critique - Jami Lin

www.jamilin.com

  1. My first impression of this website is that it is vibrant and outdated.
  2. The website includes links of different websites rather than individual pages.
  3. The structure of the site is very confusing as the flow of content does not differentiate layouts, it only uses a vertical layout.
  4. The site's graphics and layout is distracting as it has a mixed color scheme and utilizes an outdated design of graphics.
  5. The load time was reasonable, it loaded within 3 seconds.
  6. The text color and size is readable. Since the site's images had no background included, it did not detract it.
  7. The site did not include a navigation bar, it only had a home page.
  8. The color scheme was not coherent throughout the other pages and links on the site.
  9. The website was not responsive as different pages on the site did not interact with a smaller window.
  10. The website approach was unusual as it's design is very outdated.
